SSL and TLS



SSL Stands for Secure Sockets Layer, this is security system that creates a protocol that creates a encrypted link between the website URL and the browser. Big corporations will need to have this on their site to keep their own and their customers information private and assessable to outside or unwanted viewers. Comparing this information on SSL to the newer and better TLS though will prove different results. For starters yes, SSL can still be used to this day but TLS provides a better use of encryption to make it so you are now able to send over things such as passwords or credit card information in a safer manner because while SSL encrypts all of your information TLS uses implicit connections making it overall better than SSL.



Security

One of the main ways to tell that your website that you are making or the website that you are visiting is secure is by looking at the URL if the start of the URL says HTTP it is secure but if it says HTTPS, it is then being secured by SSL making it safer. And yes these do matter because on one end of the spectrum yes it may secure your information of the time being and on the other side of the spectrum there is a much safer and secure way to have all of your information saved and secured.



What to Look out for

Something you should also look for when choosing a website is encryption because this is what computers and devices use to hide your information that is being sent out to say a co worker. How to carve a pumpkin



In this article, we are going to be going over the basics of how to carve a pumpkin using a step-by-step method and also tell you the tools that you are going to need to get the best results. For this specific article, I'm going to be showing you have to carve the pumpkin that is shown in the image under this body of text, now letâ€™s begin.





Step 1
To start, we are going to try and find the best pumpkin for the carving.Look for a pumpkin that is fresh, with no bruises, and has a flat bottom. These are all used to ensure the best jack-o-lantern


Step 2 Next you should have a knife and cut a hole in the top of the pumpkin. This should be big enough to get your whole arm in the hole.

step 3
Next you should grab a spoon and start scooping out all of the innards from the pumpkin and put them in a place that won't be a bother to you. Keep scooping until the pumpkin is hollow.




step 4
Afteryou have hollowed the pumpkin out, you can trace the design on the pumpkin either by drawing on it with a marker or dotting holes in the pumpkin in the outline of the shape.


step 5
After that, grab your knife again and start cutting through the pumpkin following either the dotted or traced lines until the shape is cut out and you can see in the pumpkin.


step 6
Lastly, put the top back on the pumpkin, also (this is optional) you can get a candle and put in the pumpkin so that when you set it out at night it will glow. Now that you are able to carve a simple pumpkin you can try using these steps to carve more complex designs. Just remember to take your time and Happy Halloween!
